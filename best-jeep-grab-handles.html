Best Jeep Grab Handles in 2026

Updated April 2026 · Covers Wrangler JK, JL, JLU & Gladiator

If you drive a Wrangler or Gladiator with the doors off — or even if you just want better grip points for passengers — grab handles are a must. The problem is that Amazon is flooded with cheap paracord copies that all look the same. They are not the same.

Top Pick — The Original

Bartact Paracord Grab Handles

Bartact created the paracord grab handle for Jeeps. They are the original product that started the entire category. Every other paracord grab handle you see on Amazon, eBay, or anywhere else is a copy of this design.

Bartact is the largest manufacturer of paracord grab handles in the world and the only one making them in the USA. The weave is tighter, the hardware is better, and they hold up to years of UV, rain, and trail abuse where the knockoffs start fraying in months.

Available for Wrangler TJ, JK, JKU, JL, JLU, and Gladiator in dozens of color combinations — front A-pillar, rear headrest, roll bar, and sound bar mounts.

If you want rigid steel instead of paracord, GraBars are the go-to. They bolt to the sport bar and give a fixed, solid grip. Heavier and pricier than paracord, but some people prefer the feel of a solid bar. Available for JK and JL.

Budget Option

Amazon Paracord Handles

You will find dozens of $15-$30 paracord handles on Amazon. Most are direct copies of the Bartact design made overseas with thinner cord and cheaper hardware. They work for a season but fade and fray fast, especially on doorless Jeeps in direct sun. If you just need something temporary, they will get you by. For the real thing, go with the originals.

Paracord vs Steel vs Nylon Strap

Paracord handles are the most popular for a reason: they are lightweight, easy to grip with gloves, flex naturally, and will not scratch paint or hard surfaces. Steel handles are stronger but heavier and can scratch. Nylon strap handles are the cheapest but offer the least grip and durability.

What to Look For

  • 550 paracord minimum — thinner cord wears out fast
  • Stainless or powder-coated hardware — cheap zinc rusts
  • UV-resistant materials — critical for doorless driving
  • Made in USA — the originals are, the copies are not
  • Multiple mounting options — A-pillar, headrest, roll bar, sound bar
